# Privacy Policy — Mage Sight

**Effective date:** June 2, 2026
**Last updated:** June 2, 2026

---

## Overview

Mage Sight ("the App") is a probability calculator for tabletop wargaming
miniatures. This Privacy Policy explains how the App handles information when you
use it.

> **Short version:** Mage Sight collects nothing. All calculations run entirely
> on your device, and the App makes no network connections. There are no
> accounts, no ads, and no tracking.

---

## Information We Do Not Collect

The App does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information or
device data. Specifically, the App does not collect:

- Names, email addresses, or any account identifiers
- Device identifiers (such as advertising IDs or hardware IDs)
- Location data
- Usage, behavioral, or analytics data
- Crash reports or diagnostics transmitted off your device
- Any other personal or sensitive information

---

## How the App Works

All calculations performed by the App run entirely on your device (on-device
processing). No data is sent to any server, and the App does not make any network
connections. Any settings or values you enter stay on your device and are never
transmitted anywhere.

---

## Third-Party Services

The App does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting
services. The App is built with the React Native and Expo frameworks; in a
standalone (production) build, these frameworks do not transmit any user data
externally.

(Note: this applies to the App itself. This privacy-policy web page is hosted on a
third-party platform — see "This Website's Hosting (GitHub Pages)" below.)

page**, however, is hosted on GitHub Pages, a third-party service operated by

Specifically, when a GitHub Pages site is visited, the visitor's IP address is
logged and stored by GitHub for security purposes, regardless of whether the

the security and integrity of its service; it is not used by us for tracking,
analytics, or marketing, and we do not have access to it as identifiable visitor
data.

This processing is governed by GitHub's own policies, not ours. For details, see

and [GitHub Pages data collection](https://docs.github.com/en/pages/getting-started-with-github-pages/what-is-github-pages).
